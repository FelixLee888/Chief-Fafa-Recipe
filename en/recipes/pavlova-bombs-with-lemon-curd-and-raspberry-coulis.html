Ingredients

  • ml egg whites (from 3l
  • 2/3 cup caster sugar
  • 1 1/4 teaspoons cornstarch
  • 1/2 teaspoon white vinegar
  • 250 g raspberries (fresh or frozen)
  • 2 1/2 tablespoons white sugar (for coulis)
  • 1/2 teaspoon lemon juice
  • 3 tablespoons white sugar (for curd)
  • 1 tablespoon finely grated lemon zest
  • 3 egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
  • 50 g unsalted butter, cubed
  • 1 cup heavy cream (for whipped cream)
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons white sugar (for whipped cream)
  • 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • Chopped pistachios, fresh raspberries, mint leaves, and icing sugar for garnish

Instructions

  1. Prepare the pavlova shells 🤍 Preheat oven to 225°F (110°C) or 210°F (100°C fan).
  2. Beat the egg whites for 1 minute until foamy.
  3. Gradually add caster sugar over 30 seconds while continuing to beat.
  4. Beat for about 5 minutes until thick and glossy.
  5. Add cornstarch and vinegar, then beat for another 30 seconds until fully incorporated.
  6. Bake for 1 1/2 hours.
  7. Turn off the oven and allow the pavlovas to cool completely inside for at least 3 hours or overnight.
  8. Make the raspberry coulis 🍓 Combine raspberries, sugar, and lemon juice in a small saucepan.
  9. Simmer for 3 minutes while mashing the fruit.
  10. In a saucepan, whisk egg yolks with the lemon sugar.
  11. Add lemon juice and butter.
  12. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until thick and custard-like.
  13. Stir smooth before using.
  14. Make the whipped cream ✨ Beat heavy cream with sugar and vanilla until softly whipped and fluffy.
  15. Serve immediately for the perfect surprise burst of flavor 🎉.
  16. Prepare the pavlova shells 🤍 Preheat oven to 225°F (110°C) or 210°F (100°C fan). Line a baking tray with parchment paper.
  17. Beat the egg whites for 1 minute until foamy. Gradually add caster sugar over 30 seconds while continuing to beat. Beat for about 5 minutes until thick and glossy.
  18. Bake for 1 1/2 hours. Turn off the oven and allow the pavlovas to cool completely inside for at least 3 hours or overnight. Store in an airtight container once cooled.
  19. Make the raspberry coulis 🍓 Combine raspberries, sugar, and lemon juice in a small saucepan. Simmer for 3 minutes while mashing the fruit.
  20. Prepare the lemon curd 🍋 Rub lemon zest into the sugar to release the oils. In a saucepan, whisk egg yolks with the lemon sugar.
  21. Add lemon juice and butter. Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until thick and custard-like.
  22. Strain through a sieve, cover with plastic wrap touching the surface, and chill. Stir smooth before using.
  23. Finish with more whipped cream, chopped pistachios, a fresh raspberry, mint leaves, and a light dusting of icing sugar. Serve immediately for the perfect surprise burst of flavor 🎉.
