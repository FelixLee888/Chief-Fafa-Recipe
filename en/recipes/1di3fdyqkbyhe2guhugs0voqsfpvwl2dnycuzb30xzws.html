Grilled sea cucumber with green onion

In addition to being rich in nutrients, sea cucumbers can be cooked in different ways, so they are very flexible. This time I tried the "Japanese ginseng roasted with green onions". Although it took a lot of work, its alluring aroma of green onions is irresistible🤤

Prep: 15 min
Cook: 20 min
Total: 35 min
Servings: TBD

Ingredients

  • green onions
  • lard
  • chicken soup
  • light soy sauce
  • Old soy sauce
  • star anise
  • white pepper

Instructions

  1. Cut green onions into sections.
  2. Heat the lard in a wok, fry the onions until golden brown, and remove.
  3. Add chicken soup, light soy sauce, sugar, and wine to the original wok and steam for about 8-10 minutes.
  4. Remove the oil from the wok again, add ginger slices, green onion slices and coriander heads to make scallion oil.
  5. Boil the sea cucumbers in water for about 2 minutes.
  6. Return the remaining ginger and scallion from the fried scallion oil to the wok, add star anise, wine, and light soy sauce, sauté until fragrant, then add chicken stock to make a base soup.
  7. Add back the soup from step 3, cover with corn starch and water, and finally pour scallion oil on the plate.

  10. Add chicken soup, light soy sauce, sugar, and wine to the original wok and steam for about 8-10 minutes.
  11. Remove the oil from the wok again, add ginger slices, green onion slices and coriander heads to make scallion oil.

  13. Return the remaining ginger and scallion from the fried scallion oil to the wok, add star anise, wine, and light soy sauce, sauté until fragrant, then add chicken stock to make a base soup.
  14. Add sugar to a wok and stir-fry until brown. Add sea cucumber, scallion oil, wine, light soy sauce and base soup. Stew over low heat for about 10 minutes. Season with white pepper and dark soy sauce.
  15. Add back the soup from step 3, cover with corn starch and water, and finally pour scallion oil on the plate.
