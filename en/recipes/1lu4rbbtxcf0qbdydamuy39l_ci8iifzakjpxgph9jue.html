Ingredients

  • 7 eggs (about 60g/each)
  • Low gluten flour 105g
  • Corn oil 80g
  • Room temperature milk 100g
  • Lemon juice 7 grams
  • 70g white sugar
  • 7 eggs
  • 105g low gluten flour
  • 80g corn oil
  • 100g room temperature milk
  • 70g sugar

Instructions

  1. Sift 105 grams of low-gluten flour, add 80 grams of corn oil, and stir until it reaches 70-80 degrees.
  2. Add 100 grams of room temperature milk and continue stirring until the batter is even.
  3. Add 7-8 grams of lemon juice to the egg whites, then add 70 grams of sugar at one time, and beat until stiff peaks form.
  4. Gradually mix the meringue into the egg yolk batter, and stir gently until evenly mixed to avoid defoaming.
  5. Take a portion of the meringue and mix it with the egg yolk batter, then pour it back into the meringue, and continue mixing until a fine and smooth batter is obtained.
  6. Line a 28×28 square plate with oil paper or oilcloth, and pour the cake batter into the plate from a high height.
  7. Add a small amount of cocoa powder to the batter, stir evenly and put it into a piping bag for later use.
  8. Preheat the oven to 150 degrees and bake for 65 minutes. For the last 10 minutes, adjust the temperature to 160 degrees and add boiling water to the bottom of the oven.
  9. After taking it out of the oven, let it cool, peel off the oilcloth, trim the edges, and cut into any shape you like.
  10. Sift 105 grams of low-gluten flour, add 80 grams of corn oil, and adjust to 70-80 degrees.
  11. Add 100 grams of room temperature milk and continue stirring until the batter is even.
  12. Add 70 grams of sugar to the egg whites and beat until stiff peaks form.
  13. Gradually add the egg yolk batter, stirring carefully to avoid defoaming.
  14. Preheat the oven, use an oven with boiling water, and bake at 150 degrees for 65 minutes, increasing the temperature for the last 10 minutes.
  15. After cooling, trim edges and cut into desired shapes.
