Instructions

  1. "I really like eating shredded pork fried noodles, but they must be golden on both sides with a soft noodle core in the middle to taste good. Nowadays, most shredded pork fried noodles are deep-fried, and the middle part ends up tasteless," lamented the famous chef Ricky.
  2. To make delicious shredded pork fried noodles, first focus on the noodle cake. He said the most important step is to season the boiled noodles with light soy sauce before pan-frying. "Use an appropriate amount of oil to slowly pan-fry the surface of the noodles until crispy. Never use too much oil to deep-fry them just to save time," he said with a smile.
  3. He recommends using pork neck meat instead of lean meat because lean meat often becomes dry and tough after stir-frying, but pork neck meat avoids these problems.
  4. The secret to preventing the sauce from clumping is to mix the chicken broth with cornstarch first, then immediately pour it into the stir-fried ingredients and slowly cook until it boils. The resulting sauce will not have lumps and will have just the right thickness.
  5. Finally, place the noodles on top of the cooked ingredients. "This way, when serving, the sauce will not make the golden crispy noodle cake soggy. Crispy outside and soft inside shredded pork fried noodles are the best," he said while eating.
